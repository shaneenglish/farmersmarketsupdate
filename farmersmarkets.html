More NYC Farmers’ Markets Accepting Food Stamps

by shmcgowanenglish | Feb 22, 2016 | 0 comments

By Alyssa Pagano & Shane English

The number of farmers’ markets accepting SNAP benefits has grown from 65 to 134 since 2011 according to a New York City Department of Health report.

SNAP, Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program, commonly known as food stamps, is a government program to help supplement low-income families and decrease food insecurity.

According to the NYC DOH, 134 markets accept food stamps in the five boroughs. Typically these markets are open one day per week for a limited number of hours; Saturday and Wednesday from approximately 8 a.m.-4 p.m. are the most common days and hours of operation.

Despite the limited locations and hours, the NYC DOH’s Health Bucks program offers an incentive for families to make the trek. Using an EBT card at any of the participating farmers’ markets will net the family a bonus: for every $5 spent, they earn $2.

The points on the map below show the location of each of the SNAP friendly farmers’ markets. The map is color coded to show the percentage of residents in each community who receive food assistance from the government.

Even in the most affluent communities in the city, a small percentage of people receive SNAP benefits. Midtown East, Manhattan is on the low end, with only 2.9 percent of residents receiving SNAP assistance. On the other hand, 54.6 percent of Mott Haven, Bronx residents receive food assistance.

Despite a push from the NYC DOH to make Farmers’ Markets more accessible and affordable to low-income families, many poor communities in NYC are still far away from SNAP friendly markets.
